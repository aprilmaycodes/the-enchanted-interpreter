# The Cottage
---

You weren't expecting this.

When you got the letter that your grandma had passed, leaving you her cottage - and everything in it - you expected boxes of things to donate, stacks of paperwork to go through, maybe an overgrown garden. So you set off for the village of Willowmere anticipating tedium, and probably guilt over not visiting more often. 

The cottage was smaller than you'd expected, and much older. It sat on the edge of the village, and did, in fact, come with an overgrown garden. Inside was strangely tidy. You had prepared yourself for clutter, but the cottage was cozy and inviting. You spent the first hour trying to understand what you'd inherited. Bookshelves lined the living room walls, so you flipped through a few of the journals but they were written in a strange language. It all felt so foreign, and yet, inexplicably familiar.

You felt drawn to the kitchen, where jars of dried herbs covered the counters and a kettle sat on the stovetop. You were just about to prepare a cup, when you saw it. Peeking out from a woven rug is a handle. You slid the rug over to reveal a wooden door, the wood dark and heavy and older than anything in the cottage. It opened easily, compelling you to descend.

Where you thought you'd find a cellar you instead found a long hall. Five doorways lined the wall on both sides. You enter the first room and as your eyes adjust to the dim light, you see them - sigils carved into the stone wall, a faint, flickering blue light emanating from them.

*Something tells you your grandma didn't just leave you a cottage.*

*She left you a responsibility.*
