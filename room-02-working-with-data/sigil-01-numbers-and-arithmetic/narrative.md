# Room 2: The Supply Ledgers

The second room smells like cedar and dried lavender.

Shelves line the walls floor to ceiling, stocked with jars and bundles and crates in varying states of fullness. Some are labeled in your grandma's handwriting, the strange language from the journals. A few are simply empty.

On a low table near the entrance is an open ledger. The entries are meticulous — dates, quantities, suppliers, costs. Your grandma tracked everything. And the most recent entry, from several months ago, is circled in red with a note in the margin: *getting low.*

There's a letter tucked into the spine of the ledger.

In it, your grandma explains the supplies — what they're for, which matter most, what happens when the stores run dry. At the bottom she mentions a traveling merchant who passes through Willowmere every third market day. He's reliable, she says, but watch him. *He'll charge full price if he thinks you don't know better. You'll know better.*

*He'll offer to knock a few coins off the top of your total as a ‘neighborly favor.’ Ensure he takes that gold off first - only then let him apply his seasonal tax. A witch who pays a percentage on a full price is a witch with an empty purse.*

You set the letter down and check near the hearthstone. Sure enough, there's a loose brick that slides free, revealing a pouch of coin. Heading back to room 2 of the cellar, you prepare to meet the merchant.

*The numbers, at least, you can work with.*
